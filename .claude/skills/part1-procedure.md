# CGDCR-2017 Part I: Procedure Regulations

## Chapter 3: Development Permission Procedures

### 3.1 When Development Permission is Required
Required for: erection/re-erection/material alteration of any building; operations involving excavation; change of use; sub-division or amalgamation of building units; laying out or formation of roads or streets.

NOT required for: normal maintenance/repair; minor works not affecting structure/use; internal alterations not affecting load-bearing elements.

### 3.6 Obtaining Development Permission

**Application must include (Reg 3.6.1):**
- Site plan, building plan, service plan
- Ownership/title documents
- Latest property card/7-12 extract
- Certificates from authorized persons on record
- Scrutiny fee challan

**Scrutiny Fees (Reg 3.6.2):**
Calculated as per Schedule attached to regulations, based on built-up area and use type.
- Scrutiny fee for excess area/unauthorized work = fee calculated only for the excess/unauthorized area (Reg 3.9.5)

**Grant or Refusal (Reg 3.6.3):**
- Authority must decide within 30 days of complete application
- Deemed permission if no decision within stipulated time
- Written reasons required for refusal

**Penalties for Unauthorized Development (Table 3.4):**
Penalty = multiple of scrutiny fee based on extent of violation.

### 3.7 Revising and Revalidating Development Permission

**Revision (Reg 3.7.1):** Changes in sanctioned plan during construction; application with revised drawings + fee required.

**Revalidation (Reg 3.7.2):** Permission lapses if not acted upon within specified period. Revalidation fees per Table 3.1.

### 3.9 Building Use Permission (Occupancy Certificate)

**Application Requirements (Reg 3.9.1):**
- Completion certificate from Architect/Engineer on record
- Structural stability certificate
- Fire NOC (if required)
- Lift inspection certificate (if applicable)

**Supervisor Requirement (Table 3.2):**
Supervision required based on building height and use.

**Fees (Table 3.3):**
Fees for Building Use Permission based on use type and built-up area. Change of use requires fresh permission.

**Unauthorized Building (Reg 3.10):**
- Competent authority may regularize with penalty
- Penalty calculated on scrutiny fee basis per Table 3.4
- Structural stability certificate mandatory for regularization

### 3.11 Conflict Resolution / Appeal (Table 3.5)
Appeal committee structure for conflict resolution against authority decisions.

## Chapter 4: Persons on Record (POR)

### 4.1 Who Must Be Registered
All professionals who sign/certify drawings and documents for development permission must be registered as Persons on Record with the Competent Authority.

### 4.4 Types of Persons on Record

**Architect on Record (AROR):**
- Registered architect under Council of Architecture Act, 1972
- Can certify all types of buildings

**Engineer on Record (EROR):**
- Degree/diploma in Civil/Structural Engineering
- Various categories based on building height:
  - Category 1: up to 16.5 mts
  - Category 2: up to 45 mts  
  - Category 3: all heights

**Structural Engineer on Record (SEROR):**
- Specialized structural design certification
- Required for H3, H4 category buildings (>30 mts)

**Clerk of Works on Record (COWOR):**
- Site supervision professional
- Required for specified building heights/uses

**Supervisor of Works on Record (SOR):**
- On-site construction supervisor
- Less qualified than COWOR; used for smaller buildings

**Fire Protection Consultant on Record (FPCOR):**
- Required for buildings needing fire NOC (Schedule 11)
- Certifies fire safety compliance

**Key Rule (Amendment Jan 2019, Reg 4.1.1):**
Any person on record registered with municipal corporations and its development authority, even if not registered with any other competent authority, will be authorized to practice in the jurisdiction of those competent authorities.

### 4.5 Change of Ownership
Development permission to be withheld if ownership changes without informing authority. Fresh application or amendment required.

### 4.7 Safety Requirements for Buildings > 45 mts
Special structural/safety requirements mandated for buildings above 45 mts height.

## Chapter 5: Procedure During Construction

### 5.1 Inspections
- Authority can inspect at any time
- Key stage inspections: foundation, plinth, slab levels, completion
- Fire safety inspections during construction for specified buildings

### 5.2 Site Display Requirements
Following must be prominently displayed on site:
- Copy of development permission
- Name of owner, architect, structural engineer
- Sanctioned plan (copy)

### 5.5 Progress of Construction
- Notice of commencement of construction to authority
- Plinth completion certificate before proceeding above plinth
- Structural audit required at specified intervals (Schedule 6)

### 5.6 Structural Safety / Supervision Levels (Tables 5.1, 5.2)
Risk categorization based on consequence class:
- CC1: Low consequence (small residential)
- CC2: Medium consequence (normal buildings)
- CC3: High consequence (large public buildings, tall structures)

Supervision Level (SL) increases with consequence class.
