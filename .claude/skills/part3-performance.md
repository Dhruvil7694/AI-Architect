## Chapter 13: Buildings and Infrastructure

### 13.1 Architectural Elements

#### Boundary Wall / Compound Wall (Table 13.1)

| Sr. | Use | Road Side (max ht, mt) | Other Sides (max ht, mt) |
|-----|-----|------------------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Other than Industrial | 1.5 | 1.8 |
| 2 | Industrial | 3.0 | 3.0 |

Height measured from crown of adjacent road.

**Road junction rule:** Grill fence with ≥50% perforation; base ≤0.8 mts for 9 mts length from corner.

#### 13.1.1 Boundary Gate
- Must NOT open outward onto road/pavement.
- Minimum width: **6.0 mts** (all uses except Dwelling-1 & 2).
- At road junctions: no gate on the curvature; on roads ≥12 mts wide, gate opening ≥15 mts from corner. If road frontage <15 mts, gate at farthest end from junction.

#### 13.1.2 Level of Building Unit
- Min level = crown of adjacent road (never lower).
- Two-road access: take level from the wider road.
- Any road–unit level difference must be accommodated within the unit boundary.

#### 13.1.3 Paving in Building Unit
- Maximum **50% of total open space** (including marginal open spaces and common plot) may be paved.
- Remaining must be permeable for rainwater percolation.

#### 13.1.4 Access Path
- Min width: **1.8 mts**, even surface, no steps.
- Sloping path gradient: max **1:12**.
- **Except Dwelling-1&2:** min one accessible entrance with ramp; ramp width 1.2/1.5/1.8 mts for length 3.6/up to 9/more than 9 mts.
- Ramp specs: gradient ≤1:12, min width 1.2 mts, max continuous length 9 mts, 800 mm handrail on both sides extending 300 mm beyond top & bottom.
- Entrance landing: min 1.2 mts × 1.5 mts adjacent to ramp.
- Stepped approach (additional): min width 1.35 mts, tread ≥300 mm, riser ≤150 mm, max 12 risers without landing, 900 mm handrail both sides.

#### 13.1.5 Plinth
- Min plinth height: **0.45 mts** from established building-unit level.
- Hollow plinth: height 3.0–3.5 mts from FGL to FFL; free of enclosures except staircase and other permitted uses.

#### 13.1.6 Entrance (all buildings except Dwelling-1&2)
- Min clear door opening: **900 mm**, no step obstructing wheelchair.
- Level difference at threshold: ≤12 mm.
- Kick plates 300 mm high on glazed manual doors.
- Door handle/lock: 900–1000 mm above floor, single-hand operable.

#### 13.1.7 Minimum Clearance Height

| Situation | Min Clear Height |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Habitable rooms (Dwelling / Commercial) | 2.9 mts (floor to floor) |
| Circulation/service spaces (verandah, bath, toilet, passage, store, stair cabin) | 2.1 mts |
| Industrial — all occupiable spaces | 3.0 mts |
| Folded roof (from lowest fold) | 3.0 mts |
| Sloping roof (from lowest point) | 2.2 mts |
| Trussed roof (floor to tie beam bottom) | 2.8 mts |
| Hollow plinth (parking) — max | 3.5 mts |
| Basement (parking) — min | 2.8 mts |
| Basement (parking) — max | 4.5 mts |
| Mechanical parking basement | More height may be permitted |

#### 13.1.8 Mezzanine
- Min clear height: **2.1 mts** (below and above mezzanine).
- Area ≤ **30% of the enclosed space**.

#### 13.1.9 Loft
- Max height: **1.2 mts**.
- Min clear height below loft: **2.1 mts**.
- Area ≤ **30% of the enclosed space**.

#### 13.1.10 Basement
1. Allowed per Reg 6.8 (Part II).
2. May use available area after required margins.
3. Multiple levels permitted.
4. Min clear height: **2.8 mts** (or per Reg 6.8).
5. If used for parking → vehicular ramps per Reg 6.7.3 & 13.1.14.
6. Where basement margin < superstructure margin: no level difference in marginal space; marginal space shall be open to sky and motorable.
7. Shoring/strutting/sheet piling mandatory during construction.
8. **No drainage or water supply** provision permitted in basement.
9. Structural materials must be **fire resistant** — no wood or combustible structural members.
10. **No direct entry from road** to basement.

#### 13.1.11 Railings
- Min height: **1.15 mts** from FFL at all accessible edges (roofs, staircases, terraces, balconies, floor edges, large openings, glazed windows).
- Max perforation dimension: **0.15 mts**.
- Handrail pipe: min **40 mm diameter**.

#### 13.1.12 Terrace
- Accessible by common staircase; free from partitions.

#### 13.1.13 Staircases (Table 13.2)

| Use | Building Height | Min Flight Width | Min Tread | Max Riser | Max Travel Distance |
|-----|----------------|-----------------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Dwelling-1&2 | ≤12 mts (3 floors) | 1.0 mt | 250 mm | 200 mm | 30 mt (R) / 25 mt (NR) |
| Dwelling-3 | ≤12 | 1.2 mt | 250 mm | 180 mm | 30 (R) / 25 (NR) |
| Dwelling-3 | >12 & ≤25 | 1.5 mt | 250 mm | 180 mm | 30 (R) / 25 (NR) |
| Dwelling-3 | >25 | 2.0 mt | 300 mm | 160 mm | 30 (R) / 25 (NR) |
| Non-Residential (excl. Industrial & Assembly) | ≤25 | 1.5 mt | 300 mm | 160 mm | 25 |
| Non-Residential (excl. Industrial & Assembly) | >25 | 2.0 mt | 300 mm | 160 mm | 25 |
| Assembly | All heights | 2.0 mt | 300 mm | 160 mm | 25 |
| Industrial | All heights | 1.5 mt | 300 mm | 160 mm | 25 (NR) / 20 (HZ) |

**Alternative:** Two staircases of 1.5 mts = one of 2.0 mts; two of 1.2 mts = one of 1.5 mts (within travel distance).

**Basement staircase:** Same width as regular staircase; per Fire Prevention & Life Safety Regulation-2016.

**Mixed use:** Separate staircase if GF/any floor used for non-residential in a residential building.

#### 13.1.14 Ramp

**Vehicular Ramp (Table 13.3):**

| Vehicle | Min Ramp Width | Min Inner Curve Radius |
|---------|---------------|------------------------|
| Two-wheeler | 2.0 mts | 2.0 mts |
| Car | 3.0 mts | 3.0 mts |
| Truck | 6.0 mts | 4.0 mts |

- Max slope: **1:7**.
- Level platform at top and bottom: width = ramp width, min length **4.5 mts**.
- Min clear height on ramp: **2.6 mts**.

**Basement Ramp Count (Table 13.4):**

| Parking Area | No. of Ramps | Width |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| ≤750 sq.mt | 1 | 3.0 mts |
| >750 sq.mt | 1 | 6.0 mts |
| >750 sq.mt | 2 | 3.25 mts each |

**Pedestrian Ramp** (required for buildings >4 mts height with Institutional, Assembly, Public Institutional, Educational-2, Mercantile, Business uses — unless lift is provided per Reg 13.12):
- Width: 1.2/1.5/1.8 mts for ramp length 3.6/up to 9/more than 9 mts.
- Landing: 1.5 mts depth every 9 mts.
- Max slope: **1:12**.
- Edge protection: min 100 mm.
- Handrails: both sides at **700 mm and 900 mm** height, extending 300 mm beyond top and bottom.
- Entrance landing: min 1.8 mts at start and end.

### 13.2 Parking

#### 13.2.1 Parking to be Provided
Per Part II or Schedule 16 (Part I). Disability parking required for all buildings except Dwelling-1&2.

#### 13.2.2 Car Parking Design
- Min car space: **2.5 mts × 5.5 mts**.
- Access lane: min **3 mts** (single-sided); min **5.5 mts** (double-sided).
- Min clear height: **2.6 mts**.
- Disability parking: **2 spaces per 25 cars** (or fewer); bay width 2.5 mts + 1.2 mts side transfer bay; within 30 mts of main entrance; 50% visitor parking at ground level.

#### 13.2.3 Two-Wheeler Parking Design
- Min space: **0.90 mts × 2.0 mts**.
- Access lane: min **2.0 mts**.
- Min clear height: **2.6 mts**.

#### 13.2.4 Truck Parking Design
- Min space: **4.0 mts × 8.0 mts**.
- Access lane: min **6.0 mts**.

#### 13.2.5 Loading/Unloading Space
Required for: Industrial, Storage, Mercantile-1/2/3 (except shops ≤1000 sq.mt carpet area, restaurants, hospitals, nursing homes, business, light home workshop, IT/call center, labs).
- Rate: **3.5 mts × 7.5 mts** per 1000 sq.mt carpet area or part thereof.
- Ramp width: min **6.0 mts**.
- Permitted on ground or first basement; counts as part of parking area.

As per NBC Part VIII, Section I.

### 13.4 Ventilation

| Space | Requirement |
|-------|------------|
| Habitable rooms | Openings ≥ 1/10th floor area for open space; aggregate openings ≥ 1/7th floor area |
| Bathrooms/WC | Min 0.25 sq.mt opening per unit; open space ≥ 0.9×0.9 mts |

- Atrium covered from top: must have light & ventilation provision.
- Deficiency in all cases: compensate with mechanical systems (NBC standards).
- **No conversion** of air-conditioned theatre to non-air-conditioned.

### 13.5 Air Conditioning (Special Buildings)
- Temperature: 22–26.5°C (72–80°F)
- Air changes: ~10/hour
- Relative humidity: 50–60%
- Fresh air: 7.5 CFM/person

### 13.6 Water
- Water tank: mosquito-proof; tank >1.5 mts height needs permanent non-ferrous ladder.
- Non-residential buildings: accessible drinking water near accessible toilet.

### 13.7 Signage (all buildings except Dwelling-1&2)
- Letter size: min **20 mm**.
- Contrasting colour, illuminated.
- International wheelchair symbol at lift, toilet, staircase, parking.
- Educational/institutional/govt: Braille information board near entrance.

### 13.8 Letter Box
Required for buildings with more than **2 floors** (including GF); one per unit at ground floor level.

### 13.9 Sanitation

**Residential:** Min 1 WC of 0.9 sq.mt per dwelling unit.

**Non-Residential:** Min 1 accessible WC (1.5 mt × 1.75 mt; 900 mm door opening outward; handrails 50 mm from wall; WC seat 500 mm from floor) at ground level. Plus gender-specific WCs per Table 13.9.1:

| Use | Users Rate | Urinals | WCs | Washbasins |
|-----|-----------|---------|-----|-----------|
| Educational | 1/sq.mt carpet | 5 or 1/50 males | 2 or 1/200 users/gender | — |
| Business | 1/4 sq.mt | See table (1/25 per gender for 21–100 users) | — | — |
| Industrial/Storage | 1/25 sq.mt | 1/100 males (≤100 users); 1/50 (>100) | 1/25/gender (≤100); 1/50/gender (>100) | 4 sq.mt wash area |
| Special Buildings | 1/seat | 2/75 users | 1/100/gender | 1/200 users |

- Min **25% of WCs and urinals** in common accessible locations.

**Special Buildings:** WC count based on seats; per-gender apportionment.

### 13.10 Drainage
Subject to Competent Authority approval. Must comply with Gujarat Pollution Control Board norms.

**Septic Tank (where no public drainage):**
- Min inner width: 75 cms; min depth below water level; per capita min liquid capacity: 85 litres; length ≥ 2× width.
- Sub-soil dispersion: ≥12 mts from drinking water source; ≥2 mts from habitable building.
- Vent pipe: min 50 mm dia, mosquito-proof cage, ≥2 mts above septic tank.
- Pipe min diameter: 100 mm; junction angle ≤45°.
- Gradient of drains: 1:300 to 1:1400.

### 13.11 Electrical Infrastructure
As per NBC Part VIII, Section 2.

### 13.12 Lifts and Elevators

**Mandatory lift triggers:**
- Buildings >4 mts height with Institutional, Assembly, Public Institutional, Educational-2 uses: min **1 lift** to all public floors above GF.
- Buildings >10 mts height:

| Use | >10 mts | >25 mts |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Dwelling | Min 1, or 1 per 30 units (excl. GF + 2 upper) | Min 2, or… |
| Non-Residential | Min 1, or 1 per 1000 sq.mt BUA (excl. GF + 2 upper) | Min 2, or… |

- Buildings >25 mts: one required lift must be a **fire lift** (per Fire Prevention & Life Safety Reg-2016).

**General lift requirements:**
- From ground level or lower.
- Min capacity: **6 persons** (6, 8, 12, or 24 person sizes permitted).
- Clear door width: min **900 mm**; landing in front: min **1.8 mts × 2.0 mts**.
- Handrail: 600 mm long at 1 mts height inside cabin.
- Auto door closing: min **5 seconds**, max speed **0.25 m/sec**.
- Audible floor indicator inside cabin.

- **North facade:** entire area may be glazed (10° deviation allowed).

## Chapter 15: Special Buildings Performance Regulations

### 15.1 Visibility (Auditorium/Cinema Halls)
- Nearest seat to screen ≥ effective width of picture (ratio 1:1.33); 3/4 for CinemaScope; 1/2 for 70mm.
- Balcony line of sight: ≤30° to horizontal.
- Staggered seating preferred.
- Max angle from front seat to top of screen: **50°**.

### 15.2 Mixed Occupancy
- No mixed development with buildings >45 mts height.
- Commercial and residential must be separated by a **skip/service floor** (max clear height 2.1 mts from beam bottom to FFL; may be at any floor). *(2019 amendment)*
- Exit door width ≥ 2000 mm per 600 persons.
- Min **4 exits** per assembly space, as remote from each other as practicable.
- At least 1 seating row accessible by ramp/elevator for disabled visitors.
- Aisle width ≥ 1.2 mts; max 7 seats from aisle; cross-aisles min 1.0 mt every 10 rows.
- Fascia railings: ≥1000 mm; aisle-end railings: ≥1000 mm (≥1100 mm at foot of steps); cross-aisle railings: ≥900 mm.
- Lobby waiting areas: separated from exit ways by partition/railing ≥105 cm; exits provided at 1 person per 0.3 sq.mt.
- Seating ≥300 persons: all seats fastened to floor. Tapestry must be fire resistant.
- Row spacing: ≥850 mm back-to-back; ≥350 mm clear between seat back and front of next seat.
- Non-fastened seats permitted in restaurants/clubs: max 1 seat per 1.4 sq.mt.
- Seat width (bench): 450 mm per person.
- Stage with fly galleries: automatic sprinklers over/under stage + auxiliary spaces; proscenium fire curtain (lateral pressure: 4 kN/sq.mt); emergency closing device.
- Stage roof ventilator: ≥1/8 of stage floor area; openable from stage floor by hand and by fusible link/auto heat/smoke device.
- Proscenium wall: max 2 openings ≤2 sq.mt each with self-closing fire-resistant doors.
- Automatic smoke vents (smoke detector actuated): 8 air changes/hour above auditorium.

### 15.3 Institutional Buildings
- Facilities to move bed-ridden patients across smoke barriers when floor area >280 sq.mt.
- Min 2 exits per floor (including basements): doors to outside, stairs, ramps, horizontal exits, fire escape.
- Exits per Table Reg 22.4 corridor widths.
- Combustible construction buildings housing inmates: automatic sprinklers mandatory.

### 15.4 Public Safety
- **CCTV** mandatory in public areas of assembly buildings and entertainment venues (per appropriate authority protocols).

## Chapter 16: Structural Safety

### 16.1 Applicability
All buildings per Schedule 6.

### 16.2 Structural Stability of Existing Buildings
- Periodic assessment by expert panel (intervals per Schedule 6).
- Owner/developer/occupant responsible for repair/retrofitting.
- Competent authority can carry out work at owner's cost if non-compliant.

### 16.3 Additions and Alterations
Entire structure must conform to new-building structural/seismic requirements **unless** all three conditions met:
1. Addition/alteration itself complies with new building standards.
2. Seismic forces in existing elements not increased by >5% (or element still within capacity).
3. Seismic resistance of existing elements not decreased below new-building requirement.

### 16.4 Change of Use
If reclassified to Higher Importance Factor (IS:1893-2002): must meet new-building seismic requirements.

### 16.5 Structural Safety During Construction
- Materials/workmanship per NBC Part V and Part VII.
- Borrow pits must drain; no isolated pits that accumulate water.
- Alternative materials/methods require Competent Authority approval.
- Natural disaster retrofitting: setback/margin reductions permitted by CA case-by-case.

### 16.6 Structural Safety Tests
Expert panel may require tests (at owner's expense) with approved laboratory. Test results retained by CA for ≥2 years.

### 16.7 Design Standards

**Structural:**
- IS:456:2000 (RCC), IS:800:1984 (Steel), IS:875 Part 2 (Imposed loads), IS:875 Part 3 (Wind), IS:883:1966 (Timber), IS:1904:1987 (Foundation), IS:1905:1987 (Masonry)

**Seismic:**
- IS:1893-2002, IS:13920-1993, IS:4326-1993, IS:13828-1993, IS:13827-1993, IS:13935-1993
- Form 2A structural certificate required with building plans.

**Cyclone/Wind:** IS:875(3):1987

Always use latest revision of referenced standards.

**Structural Inspection Report (SIR):** Required for all buildings in Schedule 6; submitted in Form No. 15.

### 16.8 Display Structures
Every billboard to be designed per NBC structural safety standards.

## Chapter 17: Environmental Management

### 17.1 Minimum Distance from Water Body

| Situation | Minimum Clearance |
|-----------|------------------|
| River (no embankment) — from bank boundary to any development | 30 mts |
| Embankment of river — to any building | 15 mts (or more if prescribed by other orders) |
| Other water bodies (lake/talav, canal, nala) — to any building | 9 mts |

### 17.2 Rain Water Management

**Disposal:** Effective drainage system; no discharge into adjacent units; rain water pipes on outside walls; discharge ≤0.6 mts above street level.

**Harvesting (Reg 17.2.2) — MANDATORY for ground coverage ≥80 sq.mt:**

| Plot/Building Area | Required System |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Ground coverage 80–499 sq.mt | Percolation pit or bore recharge in marginal space |
| Building-unit 500–1500 sq.mt | Percolating well with RWH system (up to 1st aquifer) |
| Building-unit 1501–4000 sq.mt | Percolating well (up to 2nd aquifer) |
| Building-unit >4000 sq.mt | 1 percolating well per 4000 sq.mt OR Water Retention Pond (min 3,00,000 L) with 1 percolating well per 5 percolating wells |

**Rain Water Storage:** Mandatory tank for building-unit area >1000 sq.mt.

**Solid Waste Bin:**
- Residential: 10 L per DU; max 80 L per bin.
- Non-residential: 20 L per 100 sq.mt; max 80 L; air-tight lid.
- Clubs/community halls/party plots: min 4-tonne container on paved area 6 mts × 7.5 mts.

### 17.3 Grey Water Recycling

**Applicable to new construction in (Table 17.1):**

| Use | Min Built-up Area |
|-----|-----------------|
| Hospitals & Nursing Homes | >5,000 sq.mt |
| Hospitality (hotels, lodges, guest houses) | >10,000 sq.mt |
| Hostels for schools/colleges/training centres | >10,000 sq.mt |
| Community centres, banquet halls | >10,000 sq.mt |
| Commercial | >10,000 sq.mt |
| Hazardous/water-polluting/chemical industries | >10,000 sq.mt |

**System requirements:**
- Separate grey water (bathroom/kitchen) from WC waste via separate downpipes.
- Recycled water stored in separate tank (purple pipes); supplied to WC flushing, garden taps, car wash — non-potable non-contact uses only.
- Double-button cistern (dual flush) mandatory in every WC.
- No cross-connection with potable water; recycled system at lower pressure.
- Testing every **6 months**; results available to CA on demand.
- Developer must install system before selling.
- Purple-coloured piping for grey water/recycled water system.

### 17.4 Tree Plantation

| Building Unit Area | Minimum Trees |
|-------------------|--------------|
| >100 sq.mt | 3 trees per 200 sq.mt (or part thereof) |
| >100 sq.mt (up to 500 sq.mt) | 4 trees minimum + 4 per 200 sq.mt |
| >500 sq.mt | 5 trees per 200 sq.mt |

- Preferably shade-giving species from Schedule 12.
- Existing conserved trees count toward requirement.
- Tree plantation deposit payable; refundable after **5 years** (if trees maintained); otherwise forfeited for tree plantation use by CA.
- Trees planted with guards; not obstructing fire vehicle access.

### 17.5 Solar Water Heating

Mandatory for buildings >2000 sq.mt:
- Bedded hospitals & nursing homes
- Hotels, lodges, guest houses
- Hostels for schools/colleges/training centres
- Community centres, banquet halls

Also for residential detached/semi-detached units with individual DU area >200 sq.mt.

**Roof Top Solar PV (Table 17.3):**

| Category | Area Standard | Generation Requirement |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|
| Plotted Housing | >1000 sq.mt | Min 5% connected load or 20W/sqft available roof space (whichever is less) |
| Group Housing | All proposals | Same |
| Educational, Institutional, Commercial, Industrial, Mercantile, Recreational | Plot ≥1000 sq.mt (mandatory if shadow-free roof >50 sq.mt) | Same |

*Available roof area = 70% of total roof (30% reserved for residents' amenities). Area provision: 12 sq.mt per 1 KWp.*

### 17.6 Energy Efficient Buildings
- GRIHA rating (or other Govt-recognised institute) → **5% discount** on total chargeable FSI payment.
- Must apply to GRIHA before commencement.

### 17.7 Environment Impact Assessment

**EIA/Environmental Clearance from SEIAA required before development permission:**

| Project Type | Trigger |
|-------------|---------|
| Building & Construction | Built-up area ≥20,000 sq.mt and <1,50,000 sq.mt |
| Townships & Area Development | Building-unit ≥50 Ha OR built-up ≥1,50,000 sq.mt |

- Phased projects: EC required before first phase approval.

## Chapter 19: Maintenance and Upgradation

### 19.1 Maintenance of Buildings

**Classification:**
- **Class 1:** All framed structures, factory buildings, cinema, auditorium, public institutional, schools, colleges, hostels.
- **Class 2:** Masonry-wall residential buildings with height >9 mts.

Both classes require **periodic structural inspection by SEOR** (intervals per Schedule 6). SEOR submits Structural Inspection Report to CA. Owner must submit within **1 month of inspection due date**.

### 19.2 Lifts and Escalators
Owner responsible for maintenance; per IS:1860-1980, IS:6620-1972, IS:4591-1968. Maintenance schedule in consultation with lift manufacturer. Log book to be maintained. Accidents must be reported to CA.

### 19.3 Fire Prevention & Safety
Owner responsible for all fire prevention provisions (fixed systems, extinguishers, first aid, fire lifts, escape stairs).
**Buildings >13 mts height:** Fire Protection Consultant on Record inspects at intervals ≤12 months; certifies fire safety to CA.

## Chapter 21: Quality Control and Inspection

### 21.1 Applicability
- All Residential buildings >16.50 mts height.
- All Non-residential buildings.
- All buildings in Seismic Zone IV & V.

### 21.2 Inspection and Safety Certificate
Owner/developer/occupant + registered Person on Record must certify inspection and safety report per Schedule 17.
