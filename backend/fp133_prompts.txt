# CGDCR-2017 Definitions (Part I, Chapter 2)

## Key Terms

**Act** — Gujarat Town Planning & Urban Development Act 1976, or Gujarat Municipalities Act 1963, or GIDC Act 1962, or GDA Act 1957, as applicable.

**Additions/Alterations** — Any change in existing authorised building, change of use, structural change (area/height), removal of part, change to walls/beams/floors, or change to means of ingress/egress.

**Apartment/Flats** — Multi-storied residential buildings (detached or semi-detached), ground floor + upper floors, separate dwelling units with common staircase.

**Atrium** — Unobstructed multi-storied open space within a building covered from top with lightweight/glazed roof.

**Balcony** — Horizontal projection (cantilevered or otherwise), including parapet/handrail, to serve as passage or sit-out.

**Basement/Cellar** — Lower storey with at least half of clear floor height below average ground level.

**Building Types:**
- **Dwelling-1 (DW1)**: Detached dwelling unit for residential purpose
- **Dwelling-2 (DW2)**: Semi-detached, row house, tenement, farm house, cottage industry, pre-school
- **Dwelling-3 (DW3)**: Multi-storey (apartments, flats, hostels, lodging/boarding, pre-school)
- **Special Building**: Theatre, cinema, assembly hall, auditorium, town hall, exhibition hall, stadium, community hall, marriage hall
- **Hazardous Building**: Storage/handling of radioactive, combustible, explosive, corrosive, toxic materials
- **Unsafe Building**: Structurally unsafe, insanitary, inadequate egress, fire hazard, dangerous to life

**Building Height Categories:**
- H1: ≤ 16.50 mts
- H2: > 16.50 and ≤ 30 mts
- H3: > 30.0 and ≤ 45.0 mts
- H4: > 45.0 mts

**Building Line** — Control line up to which plinth of building may lawfully extend (as per TP Scheme/Development Plan).

**Building Unit** — A land/plot or combination of plots as approved by Competent Authority, excluding area falling in road alignment.

**Built-up Area** — Area covered by building on all floors including cantilevered portions and walls/columns, excluding areas specifically exempted.

**Chowk** — Fully or partially unclosed space permanently open to sky within a building at any level.

**Common Plot** — Common open space exclusive of approaches and margin, at ground level or higher levels (not exceeding 10 mts from ground level), vested in residents' society on sanction of development permission.

**Competent Authority** — Person/authority authorized by Appropriate Authority or Municipal Commissioner.

**Developer** — Person legally empowered to construct/execute work on building-unit; or owner where no such person is empowered.

**Dwelling Unit** — Self-contained unit of accommodation providing living, cooking, and sanitary facilities used by one household.

**Farm House** — Building used for agricultural/farm activity in agricultural zone, incidental to agricultural use.

**Floor Space Index (FSI)** — Ratio of total floor area of all floors to the area of the building unit. FSI = Total floor area / Building unit area.

**Gamtal** — Original settlement of a village; boundaries as demarcated by Revenue Department.

**Hollow Plinth** — Open ground floor area on columns/pillars used exclusively for parking/circulation (maximum clear height 3.5 mts).

**Loft** — Intermediate floor between two main floors, with maximum 30% of enclosing space.

**Margin (Setback)** — Open space required to be left between building and plot boundary.

**Open-to-Sky Space (OTS)** — Space open to sky, required for light and ventilation.

**Parapet** — Low wall built along edge of roof/terrace/balcony.

**Pergola** — Open structure of columns supporting a roofing grid of beams/rafters with open gaps, not enclosed.

**Plinth** — The portion of a structure between surface of surrounding ground and surface of floor immediately above the ground.

**Podium** — Continuous projecting base or pedestrian area under/around building, used for parking and vehicle movement.

**Public Purpose** — Includes: sanitary works, housing for public/workers, development of scheduled areas, drainage, transport, education, medical relief, public offices, parks, water supply, markets, etc.

**Refuge Area** — Area where persons unable to use stairways remain temporarily and await rescue during emergency.

**Road Width** — Whole extent of space within road boundaries (new road as per DP/TP); or clear average width of existing carriageway and footpaths.

**Row House** — Group of residential buildings on adjacent plots (with/without common walls), joined by common walls, with only front and rear open spaces.

**Service Apartment** — Furnished apartment with kitchen/cooking facility, available for short/long-term stays.

**Skip Floor** — Floor designated for holding occupants during fire emergencies; may be used as service areas, not habitable.

**TDR (Tradable Development Rights)** — Mechanism where tradable floor space assigned to one building can be traded for use on another building unit.

**Use Categories (Table 6.3 codes):**
- DW1: Detached dwelling, clinic (no indoor treatment)
- DW2: Semi-detached, row house, tenement, farm house, cottage industry
- DW3: Apartment, hostel, dharamshala, pre-school, affordable housing
- M1: Shop, restaurant, shopping centre, LPG godown, service establishment, clinic, fitness centre
- M2: Shopping mall, lab, nursing home, corporate office, call centre, training centre
- M3: Wholesale market, indoor hospital, diamond industry, IT activity
- EDU1: Primary/secondary/higher secondary schools
- EDU2: College, polytechnic, university
- AS1: Community hall, banquet hall, town hall
- AS2: Convention centre, exhibition hall, auditorium, planetarium, stadium, museum, wadi
- AS3: Theatre, cinema, multiplex, clubs, golf course
- AS4: Party plot, garden restaurants
- INST: Research centres, hospital (INST1: Dwellings/retail as part of educational institute up to 20% FSI)
- REG: Temples, church, mosque, gurudwara, any religious structure
- HO: Bed & breakfast, guest house, hotel, motel, serviced apartment
- SE: Auto repair, workshop, fabrication, public garage

## Chapter 13: Buildings and Infrastructure

### 13.1 Architectural Elements

#### Boundary Wall / Compound Wall (Table 13.1)

| Sr. | Use | Road Side (max ht, mt) | Other Sides (max ht, mt) |
|-----|-----|------------------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Other than Industrial | 1.5 | 1.8 |
| 2 | Industrial | 3.0 | 3.0 |

Height measured from crown of adjacent road.

**Road junction rule:** Grill fence with ≥50% perforation; base ≤0.8 mts for 9 mts length from corner.

#### 13.1.1 Boundary Gate
- Must NOT open outward onto road/pavement.
- Minimum width: **6.0 mts** (all uses except Dwelling-1 & 2).
- At road junctions: no gate on the curvature; on roads ≥12 mts wide, gate opening ≥15 mts from corner. If road frontage <15 mts, gate at farthest end from junction.

#### 13.1.2 Level of Building Unit
- Min level = crown of adjacent road (never lower).
- Two-road access: take level from the wider road.
- Any road–unit level difference must be accommodated within the unit boundary.

#### 13.1.3 Paving in Building Unit
- Maximum **50% of total open space** (including marginal open spaces and common plot) may be paved.
- Remaining must be permeable for rainwater percolation.

#### 13.1.4 Access Path
- Min width: **1.8 mts**, even surface, no steps.
- Sloping path gradient: max **1:12**.
- **Except Dwelling-1&2:** min one accessible entrance with ramp; ramp width 1.2/1.5/1.8 mts for length 3.6/up to 9/more than 9 mts.
- Ramp specs: gradient ≤1:12, min width 1.2 mts, max continuous length 9 mts, 800 mm handrail on both sides extending 300 mm beyond top & bottom.
- Entrance landing: min 1.2 mts × 1.5 mts adjacent to ramp.
- Stepped approach (additional): min width 1.35 mts, tread ≥300 mm, riser ≤150 mm, max 12 risers without landing, 900 mm handrail both sides.

#### 13.1.5 Plinth
- Min plinth height: **0.45 mts** from established building-unit level.
- Hollow plinth: height 3.0–3.5 mts from FGL to FFL; free of enclosures except staircase and other permitted uses.

#### 13.1.6 Entrance (all buildings except Dwelling-1&2)
- Min clear door opening: **900 mm**, no step obstructing wheelchair.
- Level difference at threshold: ≤12 mm.
- Kick plates 300 mm high on glazed manual doors.
- Door handle/lock: 900–1000 mm above floor, single-hand operable.

#### 13.1.7 Minimum Clearance Height

| Situation | Min Clear Height |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Habitable rooms (Dwelling / Commercial) | 2.9 mts (floor to floor) |
| Circulation/service spaces (verandah, bath, toilet, passage, store, stair cabin) | 2.1 mts |
| Industrial — all occupiable spaces | 3.0 mts |
| Folded roof (from lowest fold) | 3.0 mts |
| Sloping roof (from lowest point) | 2.2 mts |
| Trussed roof (floor to tie beam bottom) | 2.8 mts |
| Hollow plinth (parking) — max | 3.5 mts |
| Basement (parking) — min | 2.8 mts |
| Basement (parking) — max | 4.5 mts |
| Mechanical parking basement | More height may be permitted |

#### 13.1.8 Mezzanine
- Min clear height: **2.1 mts** (below and above mezzanine).
- Area ≤ **30% of the enclosed space**.

#### 13.1.9 Loft
- Max height: **1.2 mts**.
- Min clear height below loft: **2.1 mts**.
- Area ≤ **30% of the enclosed space**.

#### 13.1.10 Basement
1. Allowed per Reg 6.8 (Part II).
2. May use available area after required margins.
3. Multiple levels permitted.
4. Min clear height: **2.8 mts** (or per Reg 6.8).
5. If used for parking → vehicular ramps per Reg 6.7.3 & 13.1.14.
6. Where basement margin < superstructure margin: no level difference in marginal space; marginal space shall be open to sky and motorable.
7. Shoring/strutting/sheet piling mandatory during construction.
8. **No drainage or water supply** provision permitted in basement.
9. Structural materials must be **fire resistant** — no wood or combustible structural members.
10. **No direct entry from road** to basement.

#### 13.1.11 Railings
- Min height: **1.15 mts** from FFL at all accessible edges (roofs, staircases, terraces, balconies, floor edges, large openings, glazed windows).
- Max perforation dimension: **0.15 mts**.
- Handrail pipe: min **40 mm diameter**.

#### 13.1.12 Terrace
- Accessible by common staircase; free from partitions.

## Chapter 15: Special Buildings Performance Regulations

### 15.1 Visibility (Auditorium/Cinema Halls)
- Nearest seat to screen ≥ effective width of picture (ratio 1:1.33); 3/4 for CinemaScope; 1/2 for 70mm.
- Balcony line of sight: ≤30° to horizontal.
- Staggered seating preferred.
- Max angle from front seat to top of screen: **50°**.

### 15.2 Mixed Occupancy
- No mixed development with buildings >45 mts height.
- Commercial and residential must be separated by a **skip/service floor** (max clear height 2.1 mts from beam bottom to FFL; may be at any floor). *(2019 amendment)*
- Exit door width ≥ 2000 mm per 600 persons.
- Min **4 exits** per assembly space, as remote from each other as practicable.
- At least 1 seating row accessible by ramp/elevator for disabled visitors.
- Aisle width ≥ 1.2 mts; max 7 seats from aisle; cross-aisles min 1.0 mt every 10 rows.
- Fascia railings: ≥1000 mm; aisle-end railings: ≥1000 mm (≥1100 mm at foot of steps); cross-aisle railings: ≥900 mm.
- Lobby waiting areas: separated from exit ways by partition/railing ≥105 cm; exits provided at 1 person per 0.3 sq.mt.
- Seating ≥300 persons: all seats fastened to floor. Tapestry must be fire resistant.
- Row spacing: ≥850 mm back-to-back; ≥350 mm clear between seat back and front of next seat.
- Non-fastened seats permitted in restaurants/clubs: max 1 seat per 1.4 sq.mt.
- Seat width (bench): 450 mm per person.
- Stage with fly galleries: automatic sprinklers over/under stage + auxiliary spaces; proscenium fire curtain (lateral pressure: 4 kN/sq.mt); emergency closing device.
- Stage roof ventilator: ≥1/8 of stage floor area; openable from stage floor by hand and by fusible link/auto heat/smoke device.
- Proscenium wall: max 2 openings ≤2 sq.mt each with self-closing fire-resistant doors.
- Automatic smoke vents (smoke detector actuated): 8 air changes/hour above auditorium.

### 15.3 Institutional Buildings
- Facilities to move bed-ridden patients across smoke barriers when floor area >280 sq.mt.
- Min 2 exits per floor (including basements): doors to outside, stairs, ramps, horizontal exits, fire escape.
- Exits per Table Reg 22.4 corridor widths.
- Combustible construction buildings housing inmates: automatic sprinklers mandatory.

### 15.4 Public Safety
- **CCTV** mandatory in public areas of assembly buildings and entertainment venues (per appropriate authority protocols).

## Chapter 16: Structural Safety

### 16.1 Applicability
All buildings per Schedule 6.

### 16.2 Structural Stability of Existing Buildings
- Periodic assessment by expert panel (intervals per Schedule 6).
- Owner/developer/occupant responsible for repair/retrofitting.
- Competent authority can carry out work at owner's cost if non-compliant.

### 16.3 Additions and Alterations
Entire structure must conform to new-building structural/seismic requirements **unless** all three conditions met:
1. Addition/alteration itself complies with new building standards.
2. Seismic forces in existing elements not increased by >5% (or element still within capacity).
3. Seismic resistance of existing elements not decreased below new-building requirement.

### 16.4 Change of Use
If reclassified to Higher Importance Factor (IS:1893-2002): must meet new-building seismic requirements.

### 16.5 Structural Safety During Construction
- Materials/workmanship per NBC Part V and Part VII.
- Borrow pits must drain; no isolated pits that accumulate water.
- Alternative materials/methods require Competent Authority approval.
- Natural disaster retrofitting: setback/margin reductions permitted by CA case-by-case.

### 16.6 Structural Safety Tests
Expert panel may require tests (at owner's expense) with approved laboratory. Test results retained by CA for ≥2 years.

### 16.7 Design Standards

**Structural:**
- IS:456:2000 (RCC), IS:800:1984 (Steel), IS:875 Part 2 (Imposed loads), IS:875 Part 3 (Wind), IS:883:1966 (Timber), IS:1904:1987 (Foundation), IS:1905:1987 (Masonry)

**Seismic:**
- IS:1893-2002, IS:13920-1993, IS:4326-1993, IS:13828-1993, IS:13827-1993, IS:13935-1993
- Form 2A structural certificate required with building plans.

**Cyclone/Wind:** IS:875(3):1987

Always use latest revision of referenced standards.

**Structural Inspection Report (SIR):** Required for all buildings in Schedule 6; submitted in Form No. 15.

### 16.8 Display Structures
Every billboard to be designed per NBC structural safety standards.

### 17.2 Rain Water Management

**Disposal:** Effective drainage system; no discharge into adjacent units; rain water pipes on outside walls; discharge ≤0.6 mts above street level.

**Harvesting (Reg 17.2.2) — MANDATORY for ground coverage ≥80 sq.mt:**

| Plot/Building Area | Required System |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Ground coverage 80–499 sq.mt | Percolation pit or bore recharge in marginal space |
| Building-unit 500–1500 sq.mt | Percolating well with RWH system (up to 1st aquifer) |
| Building-unit 1501–4000 sq.mt | Percolating well (up to 2nd aquifer) |
| Building-unit >4000 sq.mt | 1 percolating well per 4000 sq.mt OR Water Retention Pond (min 3,00,000 L) with 1 percolating well per 5 percolating wells |

**Rain Water Storage:** Mandatory tank for building-unit area >1000 sq.mt.

**Solid Waste Bin:**
